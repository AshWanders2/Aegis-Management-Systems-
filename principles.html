AEIS Digital Rights Principles

These principles reflect the foundation of AEIS and ARCHE: sovereignty, autonomy, and security in digital environments.

1. Data Is Self

Data is not property to be harvested. Data is identity — an extension of personhood. Unauthorized intrusion into data is intrusion into the individual.

2. Consent Must Be Explicit

No persistence, telemetry, or extraction without informed, specific, and revocable consent. Implied consent is invalid.

3. Reciprocity of Rights

Surveillance demands accountability. If enterprises monitor consumers, consumers inherit the equal right to audit enterprises.

4. Transparency Must Be Symmetrical

Transparency cannot be one-sided. Enterprises demanding consumer disclosures must provide equal transparency in their own operations.

5. Continuity of Rights Across Mediums

Rights travel with the person — from paper to pen, laptop to phone, local storage to cloud. Technology does not erase autonomy.

6. Intrusion Prohibition

Devices and accounts are sovereign environments. Unauthorized persistence — hidden scripts, telemetry, or inspectors — is digital trespass.

Closing Statement

If it is “security” when enterprises do it, it is “accountability” when consumers do it. Autonomy is not negotiable. Rights are constant. Technology is incidental.
